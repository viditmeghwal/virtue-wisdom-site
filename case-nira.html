Case Study · Skincare & Beauty

Nira.

A skincare brand whose name means light. We built an identity where every detail catches the eye like sunlight on dewy skin — a brand confident enough to whisper.

Client
Nira
Sector
Skincare & Beauty
Scope
Identity, Mark, Pattern System, Packaging, Brand Manual
Tagline
Unveil Your Inner Light.
The Brief

A name that means light, in a category that screams.

Nira came to us with strong product science but no visual world. The skincare aisle is crowded with bright pinks, clinical whites, and copy that sells anxiety. We pulled hard in the opposite direction.

The name is Hebrew for light — or shining. So that became the brief: build an identity that glows quietly, the way real, healthy skin does. No screaming. No clinical sterility. Just a brand that says "for skin that's already beautiful."

The Mark

The dot becomes a drop.

The wordmark hides the brand's secret. We removed the dot from the lowercase i — and let it fall.

A single drop of serum. A tear of dew. A moment of light made physical. It’s the kind of detail you don’t notice at first — and then can’t unsee.

Colour System

Sage, mauve, and the colour of skin.

Two earthy primaries do the heavy work — a deep, grounded sage and a warm, lived-in mauve. Cream and bone keep the system airy. No clinical white, no neon, no compromises. The result feels grown-up and quietly feminine, like a brand that doesn’t need to introduce itself.

Sage #3F493D Primary · type & lock-ups
Bone #D7D1BE Primary · backgrounds
Mauve #7D5C4E Secondary · accent
Cream #E6E1D2 Secondary · packaging
Moss #4B544A Secondary · supporting
The Pattern

Sunlight, tessellated.

A four-pointed star — sharp, weightless, repeated. It reads as sunlight catching skin, or the glint a serum leaves behind. Used quietly: as packaging texture, divider page, or a single mark sitting at the corner of a layout.

Typography

A serif that breathes.

Display work uses a tall, gently-flared serif that echoes the wordmark’s soft forms. Body copy is set in a clean, low-contrast sans — quiet enough to let the imagery do the work, confident enough to hold instructional copy on packaging.

Packaging

A signature, on every tube.

The wordmark sits on each product like a hand-signed note. Boxes carry the droplet pattern as a translucent silhouette — visible only in the right light. Each SKU lives in its own colour: sage for restorative, mauve for daily care, cream for treatment.

“The smallest mark on the page does the loudest work. A missing dot, a falling drop — that’s the brand.”
